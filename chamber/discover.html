The Abuja City Gate is a landmark monument located at the entrance of the Federal Capital Territory (FCT) of Nigeria. It is a distinctive archway that marks the boundary between the FCT and the neighboring states of Niger and Kaduna.

Zuma Rock, which is located approximately 45 kilometers north of Abuja. Zuma Rock is a distinctive 725-meter monolith that is considered a sacred site by some of the indigenous communities in the area. It is also a popular tourist attraction, with visitors often stopping to take photographs and explore the surrounding landscape.

The central mosque in Abuja is the Nigerian National Mosque, also known as the Abuja National Mosque. It is one of the most important religious and cultural landmarks in the city and serves as the center of Islamic worship and culture in Nigeria. The mosque is located in the Central Business District of Abuja and was completed in 1984. It was built with funds donated by the Islamic Development Bank, and it has a capacity of over 10,000 worshippers.

The Shehu Shagari Way. It is a major road that runs through the Central Business District of Abuja and connects several important landmarks, including the National Mosque, the National Christian Centre, and the Federal Secretariat. It was named after Shehu Shagari, the first democratically elected president of Nigeria, who served from 1979 to 1983

The Garki Market is one of the oldest and largest markets in Abuja, Nigeria. It is located in the Garki district of the city, and it is a popular destination for both residents and visitors looking to buy a wide range of goods at affordable prices. The market has several sections, including a food section, a clothing section, a household goods section, and a livestock section
